The World Bank and International Development

The World Bank is an international financial institution that provides loans and grants to the governments of low and middle-income countries for the purpose of pursuing capital projects. It aims to reduce poverty by providing financial and technical assistance to developing countries.



Why People Should Participate in International Development

  1. Promote Global Economic Growth
    • Helps reduce poverty by creating job opportunities
    • Encourages trade and investment between nations
    • Strengthens global markets and economic stability

  2. Improve Quality of Life
    • Enhances healthcare access in underserved regions
    • Supports education and literacy programs
    • Provides access to clean water, food, and housing

  3. Foster Peace and Stability
    • Reduces conflicts by addressing economic and social inequalities
    • Strengthens diplomatic relationships between countries
    • Encourages cooperation on global challenges like migration and security

  4. Develop Cultural Understanding and Collaboration
    • Encourages cross-cultural exchange and tolerance
    • Strengthens international partnerships and networks
    • Provides opportunities for language learning and global experiences
